Hi, I’m Manank!

I'm a sophomore at UC Berkeley studying Astrophysics and Physics. If there's one thing you need to know about me, it's that I love space, from galaxies to rockets to planets and everything in between. I am currently conducting research in cosmology with the Dark Energy Spectroscopic Instrument (DESI) under Dr. Allyson Brodzeller on Damped Lyman-Alpha (DLA) systems. Along with astrophysical research, I am part of two clubs on the UC Berkeley campus, Space Technologies and Rocketry (STAR) and Space Technologies at Cal (STAC). As part of STAR, I am the operations lead, handling the logistics for launches, ensuring safety among club members, and compiling all team documentation and reports. I am also a member of the recovery subteam, ensuring proper landing of the rocket. For STAC, I am the mechanical lead for a project called Lunar Hopper, aiming to build a robot that uses hopping as its primary way for locomotive motion to traverse difficult terrains that rovers and other vehicles can not access.
I also volunteer with Science Olympiad as part of the Nationals A-Team, assisting with space science events, especially Astronomy. I primarily write tests and help supervise the space science events at tournaments.
